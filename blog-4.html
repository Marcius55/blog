Unbreakable Ones: Games Of Heroes

“If you don’t surrender, you don’t lose.” — Igor, Ukrainian Veteran

Celebrating Resilience Through Sport

From November 6th to 9th, 2023, Brussels became the stage for the inspiring “Games of Heroes” — an event honoring the strength, spirit, and cooperation of injured Ukrainian veterans and NATO allies. Held with support from NATO’s Public Diplomacy Department, 11 adaptive athletes competed, showcasing their endurance and unity across nations.

This was the first time the Games were held in Brussels, marking a milestone after nine years of effort by the organization Promote Ukraine. Veterans and military personnel with war injuries stood side-by-side with NATO representatives in a show of resilience and hope.

Spirit Beyond Limitation

“It gives us an opportunity to show the Ukrainian spirit at work.” — Vasyl Kushmuns, Promote Ukraine

According to Vice President Vasyl Kushmuns, the event is more than just a competition — it’s a message. A call to recognize the undying spirit of those who have suffered war injuries yet continue to serve or inspire others.

Events included pull-ups, rowing, ball throws, jumps, and push-ups — adapted for the different physical abilities of participants. Despite physical limitations, camaraderie and shared strength filled the air.

Motivation Through Example

“This is about setting an example for others with similar trauma.” — Igor, participant

For many, the Games were a path to healing and motivation. Igor, a participant and veteran, hopes others will feel empowered to reclaim their lives through sport. Signalman Yurii Kozlovsky echoed the sentiment, emphasizing the role injured soldiers can still play — from training recruits to working in strategic units.

Support That Echoed

Families, friends, and supporters filled the stands — their energy lifting the competitors. Ukrainian flags waved proudly, while spontaneous renditions of the national anthem filled the arena with both pride and tears.

“Even with the war, we keep smiling. There’s always hope.” — A spectator

The emotional highs and lows reflected a collective will to persevere. Though many bore scars of war, their eyes carried determination — and joy.
