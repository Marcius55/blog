Unveiling Mechelen's Spiritual Life with Father Philippe Hacour

“The mass should be really a celebration, a feast, a joy.” — Father Philippe

Discovering Mechelen's Spiritual Landscape

In the heart of Mechelen, Belgium, Saint Rumbold’s Cathedral stands as both a spiritual and architectural beacon. Father Philippe Hacour, the parish priest since September 2022, invites us into the cathedral’s story — one of faith, community, and resilience.

“I think iconic for this cathedral is the tower. It's quite a tall tower. It's also unfinished,” Father Philippe notes. The tower, a towering yet incomplete symbol, reflects the uniqueness of this spiritual site — guiding locals and visitors alike through Mechelen’s skyline.

A Universal Community

“Catholic means universal — and that is what we see in our faith community here.”

Mechelen’s evolving Catholic community is deeply international. Father Philippe highlights how people from different parts of the world come together in worship, reinforcing the universal nature of the Church. This diversity, he believes, is at the heart of Mechelen’s spiritual life.

Challenges and Visions

Creating spaces for post-Mass connection is one of Father Philippe’s top goals. “A place for people to connect, share a cup of tea, and grow as a community,” he envisions. The priest hopes this will strengthen bonds and invite new energy into the congregation.

“Imagine 100 people singing together… that you really feel it’s a celebration.”

He emphasizes joy and collective celebration in worship, wishing for more communal singing during services — transforming mass into a vibrant, spiritual festivity.

Christmas at the Cathedral

Christmas stands as the most heartfelt season at Saint Rumbold’s. Father Philippe recounts the midnight Mass with the archbishop and the emotional atmosphere filled with carols, extended visiting hours, and family-centered celebrations.

“It was a strong liturgy,” he says — a moment where faith, tradition, and community converge in the city’s sacred heart.

Faith in a Changing World

Secularization, Father Philippe admits, has impacted Mechelen’s religious participation. “Secularization is immense,” he says, noting the challenge of engaging younger generations. Yet, he remains hopeful, working to draw more young families to the cathedral’s spiritual life.

A Message of Hope and Peace

“He can really change life.” — Father Philippe, on Jesus

In his final reflection, Father Philippe expresses his wish for people to grow in faith, openness, and respect. His message resonates with warmth — urging others to embrace the transformative power of belief and community.
