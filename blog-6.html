Ildiko Bács’s Odyssey: A tale of pursuing Art, Adventure, Happiness and Gratitude away from Big and Louzy city.

Story by Sara Ntervisi, Gabriella Toth, Lotte Admiraal, Willem Poppes, Aistis Pučinskas

Inspiration from Cluj-Napoca

In the village of Cluj-Napoca, Romania, a Hungarian artist named Ildiko Bács emerges as a flame of inspiration and creativity. Since the beginning, she is the manager of Ernő Bartah’s Arkhai Sculpture Park besides her documentary art works. Journeying from different places Ildiko shares insights into their artistic voyage, personal growth, and unwavering pursuit of art and happiness.

“Cause, in Romania, in Transylvania, Hungarians and also Dutch like, German people, for a long time.”

Reflecting on their choice to settle in Cluj, Ildiko's quest for independence and self-discovery led her to the University of Kluge, seeking to break free from family influences and carve a path of her own.

A Spirit of Experimentation

“It was after high school, I did not run to university. I wanted to experiment more things.”

Embracing a spirit of experimentation and curiosity, Ildiko has a diverse experience, from painting forms in a church restoration project to working in a casino. Ildiko is also exploring the realms of theater and documentary filmmaking, each venture shaping their artistic vision and narrative. In addition, Ildiko is teaching children the magic of storytelling, her unstoppable passion for nurturing creativity knows no bounds.

“Because all my life I did what I was interested in.”

Passion Without Limits

Ildiko’s dedication to following her passions and embracing curiosity underscores her belief in the transformative power of art and storytelling in shaping lives and perspectives. In a world brimming with uncertainty, Ildiko’s journey serves as a testament to the enduring power of resilience, creativity, and unwavering optimism.

“I don't want to choose... It's not about, I can't, I don't want to because, um, I love everything.”

Ildiko is the embodiment of an artist that refuses to invalidate any kind of art, she is willing to try everything and she wants to experience as much as she can.

A Transformative Pilgrimage

One of Ildiko’s most noteworthy endeavors was their transformative pilgrimage from Cluj to London, a journey that tested the limits of physical endurance and unwavering faith in the universe. Despite facing financial hardships along the way, Ildiko discovered a profound sense of interconnectedness and learned that even in the face of adversity, one is never truly alone.

Curiosity and Creativity

Ildiko’s approach to life and art is characterized by a unique blend of humility, resilience, and boundless creativity. Their refusal to confine herself to a singular creative outlet exemplifies a spirit of curiosity and a steadfast belief in the power of trying new experiences.

“Curiosity will not let your brain die. If you are curious, then the universe will be with you.”
“I don't have, I never had a fear that, I don't have money because I never had.”

Ildiko's perspective on financial challenges in the artistic realm reflects a profound sense of resilience and adaptability.

Freedom Through Filmmaking

When it comes to Ildiko's documentary and her future documentary projects. You can feel the love and excitement she has for them due to the creativity and spiritual freedom she can have with them, no limits and no rules and realism to the fullest. Ildiko already has three documentaries in the editing process and she has the feeling of adding on them, the due date remains unknown.

Through her lens, she invites the audiences to embark on a visual journey that challenges perceptions, evokes emotions, and sheds light on the intricate tapestry of the human experience.

“There are three documentaries which I have to make to add. And, I have one, which is almost, filmed. Totally, maybe, but this is just in the editing process. You can figure it out if you need something or what could it be?”

Village Over City

“What is very annoying being in the town is your freedom. It is not yours for just a second. Your freedom. is limited by the buildings that you can't see the sky. The nature. Yeah. But the sky around you looks like a little part or there may be a little hole. And this is very annoying to me.”

Ildiko prefers life in a village, she described life in the city to be something she finds annoying. Buildings block your view of the sky, and nature feels far away. To her, real freedom exists only when you can see the open sky.

A Life Philosophy

Idiko's advice is to dare, to be nice all the time in every circumstance and to be humble. She believes that being kind and having good energy will help you change everything.

As Ildiko continues to navigate the creative landscape with a sense of curiosity, brilliance, and gratitude, her journey serves as an ode to the transformative power of art, determination, and an unwavering belief in the beauty of life's unfolding chapters.
